At its core, the work poses a question: can a machine not only mimic but also feel — translating the emotion from music into its own visual language, and linking abstract ideas to form, colour, and movement? These AI-generated interpretations are brought into a virtual world that shifts and reacts in real time to the sonic properties of the composition.



"Shadows of the Rain" is both an experiment and a reflection — on authorship, perception, and the evolving creative role of machines in the emotional landscape of art.



The application was showcased at the “Resonant Worlds” exhibition at the [Backlit Gallery of Art](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anna-shvets-10451b61_generativeai-aiart-creativecollaboration-activity-7401373451854192640-yYiP) in Nottingham and the [Brewhouse Arts Centre](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anna-shvets-10451b61_resonantworlds-aiart-immersivemusicexperience-ugcPost-7441968508214714368-b5Y9) in Burton-on-Trent, among others, as part of the EPSRC-funded project.
