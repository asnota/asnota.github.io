**King. Michał Korybut Wiśniowiecki. Part II**

His son, Michał Tomasz Korybut Wiśniowiecki, became a Polish king and was the most educated monarch of the epoch,
who spoke 8 languages. He was poisoned at the age of 33. His main motif in the 2nd part of Saga is the stylization
of the estampie theme, which receives a crystal sounding being accompanied by celesta with harpsichord,
symbolizing the beauty of intellect and knowledge, which acquired the highest priority in the information society context.
The sudden dramatic culmination is highlighted by another plucked string instrument – mandolin.

**Motrona Komorowska. Part III**

The dramatic life of Motrona Komorowska, a young orphaned noble girl whose life was mutilated by Bohdan Khmelnytsky,
the head of Cossacks' army, and who was later hanged by his son, is a testimony of a woman existence of the epoch.
The third part contains a woman's voice cry along with a following requiem singing of the choir.

https://youtu.be/1TfetUwhFVI

**And then… Part IV**

The 4th part outlines the decay of Ukraine after Khmelnytsky "sold" it to Russian Empire, having signed a pact,
humiliating for Ukrainians. The hymns were the historical testimonies of the epochs, and their major mode is in a big contrast to the repressions,
holodomors, cultural, intellectual and physical elimination of the Ukrainian nation.
The Russian Empire is represented by Russian patriotic song "Slav'sya", the Soviet Union is represented by Soviet song "Lenin is always with you!";
finally, the Orange Revolution song represents Orange Revolution, which could not prevent further tragic events,
such as kills during Euromaidan in 2014 and Russian-Ukrainian war.
A spirit of the tragedy was already in the air back in 2011.
